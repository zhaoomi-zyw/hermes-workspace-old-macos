First Impression

Erster Eindruck — Ankunft in Shanghai

The plane landed at Pudong, and the first thing that struck me was the sheer scale of the airport — a forty-minute walk just to reach baggage claim. That scale turned out to be the theme of the entire trip.

Stepping outside, everything felt purposefully fast. Electric scooters weaving silently through traffic, phones scanning QR codes for street food, a rhythm that makes European cities feel almost sleepy in comparison.

💳 Payments
Cashless Everywhere
WeChat Pay and Alipay accepted at every stall and shop. I barely touched cash the whole time.
🚵 Transport
E-Scooter City
Thousands of silent e-scooters weaving through traffic — feels chaotic, somehow works perfectly.
🍲 Food
Global Hub
Authentic Italian, Japanese, Mexican — everything. But xiaolongbao (soup dumplings) were the real highlight.

Company environment: Modern office, professional team, genuinely welcoming. From day one I was included in real discussions — not treated like a "student to be tolerated."

🇩🇪 Modernes Büro, professionelles Team, ehrlich willkommen geheißen — von Tag eins an war ich Teil der echten Arbeit.

What I Learned

Was ich gelernt habe — Arbeit & Team

I worked in the IT & AI team of a major pharmaceutical company. My project: researching what AI agents can do in a real business context — where they deliver value, where they fall short, and how a regulated industry like pharma can use them safely.

🤖 Skill
Talking to AI Agents
I used Hermes Agent daily — learning prompt engineering, task decomposition, and how to validate outputs instead of trusting them blindly.
👨‍💻 Observation
Chinese Work Culture
Efficiency and rigour together. Meetings were focused, decisions quick, and every deliverable was double-checked. Nothing was done carelessly.

Talking to an AI agent is a skill you have to practise — just like learning a new language for a new world.

— The most practical takeaway from the internship

Mindset shift: Back in Germany I was used to a more relaxed pace. Here I learned that speed and thoroughness don't have to be opposites. You can move fast and still be precise.

🇩🇪 Neue Erkenntnis: Schnelligkeit und Genauigkeit schließen sich nicht aus — beides gleichzeitig ist möglich und das Ziel.

Cultural Observations

Kulturelle Beobachtungen — Leben in Shanghai

A few weeks in Shanghai gave me a crash course in Chinese urban culture. Here are the things that stayed with me:

⏰ Daily Life
Pace of Living
Everything moves faster — walking, eating, working. But it's not frantic; it's purposeful. People are in motion without being stressed.
🌐 Language
English Works Here
Getting around without Chinese was surprisingly easy. English signage, apps with English modes, and locals used to international visitors.
🥙 Food Culture
Truly International
Shanghai is a global food city. Every cuisine is authentic and widely available — the city lives up to its reputation as a world hub.
📱 Digital Life
Super-App World
WeChat is not just messaging — it's payments, bookings, social media, work chat, all in one. Makes Europe's fragmented app ecosystem feel clunky.

What surprised me most: How easy it was to adapt. I expected culture shock, but Shanghai is so international and well-connected that I felt comfortable within days.

🇩🇪 Größte Überraschung: Wie leicht die Eingewöhnung war. Shanghai ist so international, dass ich mich nach wenigen Tagen zuhause gefühlt habe.

AI Tools I Tried

KI-Tools, die ich ausprobiert habe

My main tool was Hermes Agent (open-source, by Nous Research) — I evaluated what a modern AI agent framework could do in a pharmaceutical business context.

🤖
Hermes Agent
Tested instruction following, tool use, document analysis. Learned how to design agent workflows for real business tasks.
📝
Prompt Engineering
Breaking complex tasks into step-by-step agent instructions became second nature. Precision in prompts = quality in outputs.
🔍
LLM Evaluation
Assessed models for pharma use cases — compliance, document summarisation, structured data extraction.
⚙️
Workflow Design
Mapped where AI agents add value in a regulated industry — and where human oversight stays essential.

The balance I learned: AI handles the heavy lifting, humans do the final check. In pharma, accuracy and traceability matter more than speed. That balance — efficiency without blind trust — is the real skill I'm taking home.

🇩🇪 Das Gleichgewicht: KI macht die Vorarbeit, der Mensch prüft das Ergebnis. In der Pharmaindustrie zählen Genauigkeit und Nachvollziehbarkeit mehr als Geschwindigkeit.
